# WACS Curriculum Topics for CME System

## Part I - Primary Examination Topics

### A. Principles of Surgery in General

1. **Preoperative Management**
   - Patient assessment for anaesthesia and surgery
   - Applied physiology/anatomy/biochemistry (respiratory, cardiovascular, renal)
   - Principles of anaesthesia
   - Fluid and electrolyte balance; shock
   - Laboratory investigations (biochemistry, haematology/transfusion, microbiology)
   - Imaging and interventional radiology (X-ray, ultrasound, CT, MRI)
   - Management of comorbid conditions (malaria, malnutrition, cardiovascular, respiratory, endocrine disorders, anaemia, HIV/AIDS)

2. **Perioperative Care**
   - Post-operative complications
   - Metabolic response to injury
   - DVT prevention and management
   - Renal failure
   - Sepsis, asepsis, sterilisation and disinfection
   - Surgical site infection prevention
   - Antibiotics in surgery

3. **Theatre and Trauma**
   - Theatre design and management
   - Surgical diathermy
   - Trauma basics: initial resuscitation, venous access, airway (including tracheostomy)
   - Wound exploration, wound care, wound repair principles
   - Fracture management
   - Day-care surgery fundamentals

4. **Professional Skills**
   - Surgical ethics, informed consent, medico-legal aspects
   - Communication skills, report writing and presentation skills
   - Hospital administration/management basics
   - Computers and the Internet in surgical practice
   - Surgical audit

### B. Specialty-Specific Introductory Items

1. **General Surgery**
   - Common abdominal presentations
   - Soft tissue emergencies
   - Emergency surgical conditions

2. **Trauma & Accident & Emergency**
   - Initial assessment and resuscitation
   - ATLS principles
   - Triage systems

3. **Urology**
   - Principles of renal ultrasonography
   - Bladder outlet obstruction
   - Prostatic conditions
   - Urethral/testicular problems

4. **Orthopaedics**
   - Traction/immobilisation
   - Bone & joint infections
   - Bone tumours basics

5. **Anaesthesia/Critical Care**
   - Local, regional, general anaesthesia
   - Analgesia principles
   - ICU principles

6. **Paediatric Surgery Basics**
   - Paediatric assessment
   - Hernias/hydroceles
   - Stoma care
   - Common childhood tumours

7. **Plastic & Reconstructive Basics**
   - Principles of reconstructive surgery
   - Basic burns management
   - Common craniofacial abnormalities (e.g., cleft lip & palate)
